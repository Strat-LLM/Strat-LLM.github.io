TL;DR

Strat-LLM investigates the reliability boundaries of Large Language Models in dynamic, multimodal decision-making environments. Validated through a live-forward 2025 framework to preclude data leakage, it employs Stratified Strategy Alignment to modulate model autonomy. The study uncovers a fundamental tension: while strict alignment enhances robustness against hallucinations in efficient markets, it may constrain adaptability in inefficient ones, offering key insights for deploying dependable agents in high-stakes scenarios.

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) are rapidly evolving into autonomous trading agents capable of complex decision-making; however, existing benchmarks often overlook strategy consistency and decision transparency. To address this, we propose Strat-LLM, a framework grounded in Stratified Strategy Alignment. Distinct from retrospective backtesting, our framework operates in a live-forward setting throughout the 2025 experimental window, integrating sequential numerical market streams with a static library of textual annual report summaries. This setup strictly mimics the chronological flow of information to eliminate look-ahead bias. By auditing decision rationales and confidence scores across Free, Guided, and Strict autonomy modes, our extensive stress tests on A-share and U.S. markets reveal critical insights: (1) Strategic constraints act as a double-edged sword: while they induce an "Alignment Tax" in inefficient markets (A-share) by limiting upside potential, they provide essential robustness in efficient markets (U.S.). (2) LLMs exhibit "Cognitive Dissonance" under strict constraints, evidenced by a surge in confidence volatility. (3) Performance is intrinsically linked to Market Efficiency. (4) Capability scales nonlinearly, with intermediate-scale models achieving the optimal balance between cost and performance.
